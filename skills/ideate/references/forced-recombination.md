# Forced Recombination ("Collision-Zone Thinking")

**Core operation:** Systematically collide unrelated domains through "what if X was Y" framing to generate novel possibilities.

## Why It Works

Creativity research shows breakthrough ideas rarely emerge from incremental improvement within a domain—they come from importing structures from elsewhere. This skill externalizes that process.

## This Framework In Context

Forced Recombination is one operation among many. Its job is to surface unexpected mappings, not to produce a complete answer to the problem on its own. Refining and stress-testing what emerges are separate operations:

- Use **Bounded Exploration** to prevent it from going too wild.
- Use **Inversion** afterward to stress-test what emerged.

## Process

1. **Name the stuck domain** — What are you trying to solve?
2. **Select a wildly unrelated domain** — The more different, the better
3. **Force combination:** "What if A was B?"
4. **Map where the metaphor holds** — What transfers?
5. **Test each mapping** — Could the insight exist without the source domain? If yes, it's decorative. If no, it's load-bearing.
6. **Identify the load-bearing mapping** — Of the mappings that hold, which one produces the insight you couldn't reach without the collision? That's the one to argue.
7. **Map where it breaks** — Often equally valuable. Test metaphor boundaries rigorously. Breaks reveal what the target domain has that the source lacks, or what false constraints you imported.
8. **Extract transferable structures** — What did you learn?

## Applications

A supply chain problem collided with a cardiovascular system might surface flow optimization and bottleneck detection. A software interface collided with a conversation might surface dialogue patterns and turn-taking. Entering a market collided with terraforming might surface long-term transformation under hostile conditions. A math concept collided with a dance might surface embodied understanding and rhythm.

## Red Flags You Need This

- "I've tried everything in this domain"
- Solutions feel incremental, not breakthrough
- Stuck in conventional thinking
- Need innovation, not optimization

## Tips

- Wild combinations often yield best insights
- Document even failed collisions (they teach)
- Best source domains: physics, biology, economics, psychology, games, nature
