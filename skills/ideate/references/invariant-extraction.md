# Invariant Extraction ("Pattern Synthesis")

**Core operation:** Identify what *persists* across variations—the signal that survives noise.

## Why It Works

What survives multiple passes is structurally important. Evolution works this way—beneficial traits persist; neutral traits drift; harmful traits disappear.

## Five Functions

1. **Invariant Detection** — What stays constant across iterations?
2. **Structure Extraction** — What forms repeat?
3. **Noise Filtering** — What's meaningful vs. irrelevant?
4. **Meta-Pattern Recognition** — Patterns within patterns
5. **Generalization** — Moving from specific to universal

## Process

1. **Gather variations** — Collect multiple instances of the phenomenon
2. **Compare systematically** — What's the same? What's different?
3. **Identify invariants** — What persists across all variations?
4. **Test the invariant** — Does it hold in new cases?
5. **Name the pattern** — Give the invariant a handle

## Applications

| Context | What to Extract |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Learning from failure** | What recurs across your mistakes? That's the root cause. |
| **Skill development** | What do you always do before your best performances? That's your warm-up ritual. |
| **Relationship patterns** | What dynamics recur across relationships? That's your attachment style. |
| **Project post-mortems** | What problems reappear across projects? That's organizational DNA. |

## Signal vs. Noise Test

- **Signal:** Appears across 3+ variations in different contexts
- **Noise:** Appears once or only in similar contexts
- **Weak signal:** Appears twice—needs more data

## Red Flags You Need This

- "This situation is unique" (probably not)
- Can't explain why something keeps happening
- Reinventing the wheel across domains
- "Haven't we seen this before?" (yes, find the pattern)
