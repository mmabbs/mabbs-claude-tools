# Inversion ("Pre-mortem Thinking")

**Core operation:** Flip the question. Instead of asking how to succeed, ask how to guarantee failure—then avoid those things.

## Why It Works

We're better at spotting danger than imagining success. Inversion exploits that asymmetry. Avoiding stupidity is easier and more reliable than seeking brilliance. As Charlie Munger said: "Invert, always invert."

## Process

1. **State your goal** — What are you trying to achieve?
2. **Invert the question** — "How could I guarantee this fails?"
3. **List failure modes** — Be specific, vivid, and honest
4. **Flip each failure into a safeguard** — What prevents it?
5. **Check your plan** — Does it address the top failure modes?

## Pre-mortem Variant

1. **Assume the project has already failed** — It's 6 months from now. It's dead.
2. **Write the post-mortem** — What killed it?
3. **Rank the causes** — Which were most likely?
4. **Prevent the top killers now** — Before they happen

## Applications

| Context | Inversion Question |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Product launch** | How could we guarantee nobody uses this? |
| **Hiring** | How could we hire the worst possible person? |
| **Habit change** | How could I guarantee I quit within a week? |
| **Relationship** | How could I destroy this trust completely? |
| **Writing** | How could I make this maximally boring? |
| **Strategy** | How could a competitor destroy us? |

## Red Flags You Need This

- "I think this plan is solid" (overconfidence)
- "What am I missing?" (vague anxiety without structure)
- About to make a big commitment or irreversible decision
- Everything feels too smooth — nothing has been stress-tested

## Inversion Prompts

- "What would make this a guaranteed disaster?"
- "If I wanted to sabotage this, what would I do?"
- "What's the most likely way this dies quietly?"
- "What would my harshest critic point to?"
- "What am I avoiding thinking about?"

## Remember

- Avoiding known failure modes is often higher ROI than chasing uncertain success
- The pre-mortem is more honest than the post-mortem — hindsight bias hasn't kicked in yet
- Inversion works best when you're feeling confident — that's when blind spots are widest
- Pair with `bound` to stress-test the edges after you've identified the failure modes
