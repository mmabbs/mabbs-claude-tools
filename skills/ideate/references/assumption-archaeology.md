# Assumption Archaeology ("Critical Perspective")

**Core operation:** Structured questioning using three techniques: assumption surfacing, perspective shifting, and gap identification.

## The Three Questions

1. **Assumption surfacing:** "What am I taking for granted here?"
2. **Perspective shifting:** "How would [stakeholder X] see this differently?"
3. **Gap identification:** "What's missing that will become obvious later?"

## State-Based Priority

When to apply this framework hardest:

| State | Priority | Reason |
|-------|----------|--------|
| **Biased** | Highest | Tunnel vision risk highest |
| **Focused** | High | Framework blindness |
| **Dispersed** | Moderate | Prune irrelevant threads |
| **Diversified** | Low | Avoid analysis paralysis |

## Applications

| Context | Question |
|---------|----------|
| **Planning** | What am I assuming won't change? |
| **Hiring** | What do I assume "good candidate" means? |
| **Product** | What convenience does my solution rely on? |
| **Relationships** | What am I assuming the other person wants? |
| **Strategy** | What am I assuming about competitors? |

## Dig Deeper Prompts

- "Why do I believe this must be true?"
- "When did I decide this was the only way?"
- "Who benefits from this assumption staying unexamined?"
- "What would I do if I learned this wasn't true?"

## Gap Detection

Look for:
- **Temporal gaps:** What will change over time?
- **Stakeholder gaps:** Whose perspective isn't represented?
- **Information gaps:** What data am I missing?
- **Scenario gaps:** What edge cases haven't I considered?

## Red Flags You Need This

- "There's only one way to do this"
- Feeling forced into a solution
- Can't articulate why an approach is necessary
- "This is just how it's done"
