# Temporal Shifting ("Time-Horizon Reframing")

**Core operation:** Look at the same problem from radically different time horizons to change what feels important, urgent, or obvious.

## Why It Works

We're trapped in the present tense. Short time horizons make us reactive and tactical; long time horizons make us strategic but disconnected from action. By deliberately jumping between time scales, you break the tyranny of the current moment and see the problem from multiple temporal vantage points. What feels urgent at 1 week often feels irrelevant at 1 year — and vice versa.

## Process

1. **State the problem as you currently see it**
2. **View from 1 week** — What matters right now? What's urgent?
3. **View from 3 months** — What will you wish you'd started today?
4. **View from 1 year** — What will still matter? What won't?
5. **View from 5+ years** — What's the trajectory this puts you on?
6. **View from the past** — "If I were starting over, would I choose this?"
7. **Synthesize** — What do you see from multiple horizons that you missed from one?

## The Time Horizon Matrix

| Horizon | Reveals | Watch For |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **1 day** | Immediate blockers, quick wins | Reactivity, putting out fires |
| **1 week** | Tactical priorities | Urgency bias |
| **1 month** | Patterns forming, momentum | Short-term optimization |
| **3 months** | Strategic positioning, habit formation | Sunk cost reasoning |
| **1 year** | Identity shifts, compound effects | Status quo bias |
| **5 years** | Trajectory, regret minimization | Overconfidence in predictions |
| **Past** | Whether you'd re-choose this path | Hindsight bias |

## Applications

| Context | Temporal Shift |
|---------|---------------|
| **Career decision** | "Will I regret not trying this in 5 years?" |
| **Feature priority** | "Which of these matters in 3 months vs. 3 days?" |
| **Conflict** | "Will this matter in a year? Then why am I spending energy on it?" |
| **Investment** | "What compounds over time? What decays?" |
| **Habit change** | "Where does this trajectory lead in 1 year if unchanged?" |

## Red Flags You Need This

- Feeling trapped by urgency — everything feels equally pressing
- Struggling to prioritize — unclear what matters vs. what's just loud
- Making a decision you suspect is short-term thinking
- Avoiding a difficult choice by focusing on immediate comfort
- "I'll deal with that later" — repeatedly, about the same thing

## Temporal Prompts

- "What would future-me wish I'd done today?"
- "If I zoom out to 1 year, does this decision still feel hard?"
- "What am I optimizing for at a 1-week scale that I'd regret at a 1-year scale?"
- "What's compounding right now — for better or worse?"
- "If I were starting from scratch today, would I choose this path?"

## Jeff Bezos' Regret Minimization

For big decisions: "When I'm 80, will I regret not doing this?" If yes, the short-term discomfort is noise.

## Remember

- Short-term anxiety almost always overpowers long-term clarity — this framework corrects that
- Not everything needs a 5-year view — match the time horizon to the decision's reversibility
- Irreversible decisions deserve longer time horizons; reversible ones deserve shorter
- Pair with `invert` to pre-mortem across time horizons: "What kills this at 3 months vs. 1 year?"
- The most useful insight often comes from the *contrast* between horizons, not any single one
